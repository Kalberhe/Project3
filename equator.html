Warming Across the Pacific. 5°S to 5°N by decade

Temperature anomalies near the equator between 5° South and 5° North shown decade by decade from bottom to top. Each horizontal band is a decade from 1850 at the bottom to 2010 at the top. Longitude runs left to right across the Pacific. Data transformation: Anomalies are calculated relative to the 1850-1900 baseline period. Values represent decade-wise mean temperature anomalies (°C) aggregated from monthly CMIP6 model outputs. The diverging color scale (Red-Blue) uses red for positive anomalies (warming) and blue for negative anomalies (cooling), centered at zero.

Interactions: Drag to select area and zoom in • Mouse wheel to zoom in/out • Double-click to reset zoom
